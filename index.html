Projects

Just Energy Transition in the Industries of Bangladesh (JET)

Funded by TARA Foundation, this project analyzed renewable energy policies, specifically solar and wind adoption in the RMG sector of Dhaka. Recommendations were made to ensure the transition creates green jobs and provides training for the impacted workforce.

Empowering Women through Civil Society Actors (EWCSA)

Funded by the EU, this initiative focused on empowering women through the engagement of civil society actors in Bangladesh, addressing gender inequality through community-driven actions.

Loss and Damage: Participatory Mapping

Funded by Sida, the project built a participatory map to document responses to loss and damage across Bangladesh. It engaged local communities, especially farmers, to understand their challenges, particularly regarding agricultural irrigation.

Climate Adaptation through Community Stewardship in Ecosystem Services Restoration and Management (CACS)

Funded by Oxfam Hong Kong, this project developed research frameworks and tools for sustainable forest resource management, utilizing GIS and RS analysis to support ecosystem services restoration and management.

Climate Risk Insurance (CRI) for Agriculture

Funded by FAO, this project implemented climate threshold-based agricultural risk insurance in vulnerable regions of northern Bangladesh. It included collecting digital evidence to assess the effectiveness of this insurance model.

Youth-Led Climate Initiatives in the Asia-Pacific Region

Mapped youth-led climate initiatives through surveys and interviews, publishing insights to boost youth participation in climate policy discussions and action within the Asia-Pacific region.

Gender and Climate Justice: Impacts of Environmental Policy on Women

This project assessed the gendered impact of environmental policies through interviews and reports, offering gender-responsive climate recommendations for international forums and policymaking.

Impact of Climate Change on Occurrence and Distribution of Animal Diseases and Deaths in Smallholder Farming Systems: An Investigation of Farm-level Experiences

This research explores the impact of climate change on the occurrence and distribution of animal diseases and deaths in smallholder farming systems, emphasizing zoonotic diseases within the One Health framework. It investigates farm-level experiences to understand climate-driven disease dynamics and their implications for human, animal, and environmental health.

Assessment of Indigenous Knowledge and Technology Used for Climate Adaptation and Resilience in Bangladesh

Evaluated indigenous knowledge and technologies for climate adaptation in Bangladesh, highlighting traditional resilience strategies and integrating them into national adaptation frameworks.

Medical Technology for SARS-CoV-2 Rapid Antigen Test Evaluation

Evaluated the diagnostic performance of SARS-CoV-2 rapid antigen tests in households, contributing to improved diagnostic approaches and aiding pandemic management efforts.

MICROPLASTICS IN INDOOR ENVIRONMENT IN DHAKA, BANGLADESH

This study investigates atmospheric microplastics in indoor environments in Dhaka, Bangladesh, highlighting their composition, sources, and potential health risks. Using FT-IR, SEM, and EDS, the research identified polyethylene and low-density polyethylene, along with toxic elements like Pb, Zn, and Ni. Findings emphasize the risk of bioaccumulation through inhalation, posing long-term health concerns.
